Where is the headroom?

Within each model family, an oracle picks the best variant for every dataset. The remaining gap to PLS measures how much signal that family contains across the search space — independent of how you would actually deploy it. TabPFN, AOM-PLS and AOM-Ridge dominate the ceiling.

Reading Bars left of the dotted line beat the best PLS observed for the dataset; right means worse. IQR shows variability across datasets.
Figure 2 · Forest plot

Which models are reliable?

Best per (model, dataset), then median + IQR across datasets. Each model is judged against the PLS that shipped in its own source run — fair within-protocol view that surfaces fast linear baselines like AOM-PLS without penalising them for not having tuned PLS counterparts.

Toggle Switch Comparison at the top to swap the denominator: protocol-local PLS (this view) or the strict best-PLS-ever-observed denominator (Figure 3).

Specialists, generalists, and brittle wins

Figure 4 · Win census

How often, not just how much.

For each top model, how many of its evaluated datasets undercut the corresponding PLS? Median ratio answers by how much; this chart answers how often. Models with high win rate but only modest median lift are the candidates worth defaulting to.

Reading Green segment is wins (rel < 1.0), terracotta is losses. Numbers on the right are wins / n.
Figure 5 · Pareto view

Accuracy at what cost?

Median fit time (log seconds) versus median relative RMSEP. Lower-left is the frontier; bubble size is the dataset count. Linear models cluster bottom-left at sub-second budgets; TabPFN sits higher on the time axis but matches accuracy. Filled markers along the bottom envelope are non-dominated.

Use When deciding between candidates of similar accuracy, prefer the one further left.

Methodology

Source rows are record_type ∈ {observed, reference_paper}, regression task, RMSEP-scored, status OK. For each model we keep the best row per dataset, then aggregate across datasets. Two normalisations: score_ratio_vs_source_run_pls (protocol-local) and score_ratio_vs_dataset_pls (strict cross-protocol).

Ratios < 1 outperform PLS. The heatmap clips log₂ to ±1 for legibility. Pareto excludes models with fewer than eight datasets or no recorded fit time.
